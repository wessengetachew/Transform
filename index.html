About This Visualization

Modular Lifting Rings: This tool visualizes the structure of modular arithmetic across multiple moduli simultaneously. Each ring represents Z/MZ, the integers modulo M, with points showing residues coprime to M (where gcd(r,M) = 1).

Farey Fractions & Reduced Residue Channels: Each coprime residue r in modulus M corresponds to a Farey fraction r/M in lowest terms. These reduced fractions form distinct "channels" through the nested ring system. The Farey sequence F_M contains all irreducible fractions with denominator ≤ M, creating a natural filtration of the rational numbers.

Gap Analysis: Gap connections highlight residue pairs (r₁, r₂) where |r₁ - r₂| = g for a chosen gap g. This reveals the admissible gap structure within each modular ring. A gap is "admissible" when both r and r+g are coprime to M, forming valid channels. Think of it like terminals: if both endpoints are coprime (open terminals), the gap connection is valid. If either is non-coprime (closed terminal), that gap is blocked for that configuration.

Lift Connections: Direct lifts (r → r, M → M+1) show how residues naturally embed from one modulus to the next when gcd(r,M) = gcd(r,M+1) = 1. Dyadic lifts (r → r+M, M → M×2) explore the doubling structure for consecutive moduli only, connecting a residue r in M to r+M in the next doubled modulus M×2.

Dirichlet Characters: The coprime residues (cyan points) form the domain where Dirichlet characters χ(r) are non-zero. These characters are crucial for L-functions and the Generalized Riemann Hypothesis, as they extend the classical zeta function to arithmetic progressions.
